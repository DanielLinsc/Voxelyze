00236 STRAIN ENERGY of the bond is the area under the force-displacement curve that can be recovered.
00237 
00238 PURE TENSION AND COMPRESSION:
00239 For our purposes, even with non-linear materials, it is assumed materials will rebound with their base elastic modulus.
00240 
00241 Strain energy is the shaded area, slope is EA/L, therefore bottom edge length is FL/AE.
00242 Area is then Fcurrent*(FL/AE)/2. (or, given EA/L is stiffness k, strain energy = 0.5*F^2/k (or F^2/(2k))
00243 http://www.roymech.co.uk/Useful_Tables/Beams/Strain_Energy.html
00244 http://www.freestudy.co.uk/statics/complex/t5.pdf

00258 PURE TORSION
00259 For rotational, the process is similar: T^2/(2k) where K is units of N-m

00261 BENDING
00262 The strain energy from bending = integral [M(x)^2 dx / (2EI)].
00263 Because we only load the ends of the beam, M(x) = (M2-M1)*x/L+M1 (Linear interoplation, M1 and M2 are the moments at each end).
00264 Plugging and chugging though the integral, we get:
00265 
00266 Strain energy = L/(6EI) * (M1^2 + M1*M2 + M2^2)
00267 
00268 Note that the sign of M2 must be reversed from that calculated by the stiffness matrix.
00269 This is because a positive "Moment" on a beam (in the moment diagram) is actually two opposite-direction torques on the ends of the beam. ALA http://www.efunda.com/formulae/solid_mechanics/beams/sign_convention.cfm
00270 
00271 This means Strain energy = L/(6EI) * (M1^2 - M1*M2' + M2'^2)
00272 
00273 SHEAR
00274 Not currently modeled in voxcad.
00275 
00276 SUPERPOSITION:
00277 We assume each of these modes is independent, thus total strain energy is simply the sum of them all.
